Kubbah is a traditional Syrian dish made of bulgur wheat and meat, often served in a soup or as fried patties. It is a beloved comfort food in Syrian cuisine.

Ingredients for Kubbah

  • 1 cup of fine bulgur wheat
  • 1/2 pound of ground beef or lamb
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1 teaspoon of allspice
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Oil for frying (if making patties)
  • For the soup: 4 cups of beef or vegetable broth
  • Fresh herbs (parsley or mint) for garnish

Instructions for Kubbah

  1. Soak the bulgur wheat in water for about 30 minutes, then drain and squeeze out excess water.
  2. In a bowl, mix the soaked bulgur with ground meat, chopped onion, allspice, salt, and pepper until well combined.
  3. Shape the mixture into small balls or patties.
  4. If making soup, bring the broth to a boil and add the Kubbah balls. Cook for about 20 minutes until they float to the surface.
  5. If frying, heat oil in a pan and fry the patties until golden brown on both sides.
  6. Serve hot, garnished with fresh herbs.
